Tottenham Hotspur

  • Founded in 1882, Tottenham Hotspur has been competing in the top leagues of English Football for around 140 years
  • Totenham was one of the 5 major clubs that pushed to for the creation of the now Premier League and has not been relegated from this top flight of English football
  • Currently under the guidance of Italian manager Antonio Conte, Spurs finished in 4th place in the 2021-22 season, qualifying for the Champions League for the first time several years
  • Led by a solid defense and an extremely talented front line, Spurs has been one of the leaders of the Premier League so far in the 2022-23 season
  • Spurs are looking to return to their prime and win trophies in the near future, having not won any major trophies in over a decade

Key Players

Heung-Min Son

In 2015, Son joined Spurs from Bayern Leverkusen for 30 million euros, after 2 years with the club. Since then Son has grown to be one of the faces of the the club. The South Korean winger/striker has scored 134 goals accross his Spurs career, winning a variety of awards along the way, including a Puskas award, a Premier League Golden Boot, and several Asian and Korean Player of the Year awards.

Harry Kane

Kane is a homegrown star striker out of Tottenham, emerging from the youth program. He made his first appearance for the team in 2014, and has been a star ever since. In the past 8 years, the English international and captain has scored 255 club goals and has won many awards including several Premier League and World Cup Golden Boots, as well as many Premier league Player of the Months.

Hugo Lloris

Lloris spent the early years of his goalkeeping career at Nice and Lyon in his home country of France before moving to Tottenham in 2012 for about 15 million euros. Since then he has been Tottenham's top choice keeper and continues to perform for the club.

Pierre-Emile Hojberg

Hojberg started his career with the German side Bayern Munich, before moving to Southampton and then Tottenham in 2020. The Danish international quickly became a fan favorite at Spurs, grabbing the starting spot and dominating the midfield every week. The midfielder has been a key part of Tottenham's stronger possession and defensive strength since joining the club.

Dejan Kulusevski

Kulusevski joined Tottenham from Italian side Juventus in January 2022 as a loan. The Sweedish international winger quickly made his mark on the club by racking up goals and assists, helping to bring Spurs up to a Champion's league spot after sitting in 9th upon joining. Kulusevski is a young and rising star at just 22 years old with many fans hoping for greatness out of the player.

Christian Romero

Romero joined Tottenham from Atalanta on loan in January 2022 and has since been permanently signed. The Argentinian center back has become a strong force in the back, bringing strength and composure to the back 3 of Spurs defense. He is only 24 years old, especially young for a center back, so the club hopes to build a strong defensive force around him for years to come.

Tottenham Stadium

Construction of the Tottenham stadium ended in 2019 after being initially announced in 2008, with the intent of replacing the old "White Hart Lane" This new stadium still does not have a name and is know as Tottenham Hotspur stadium. This new stadium has a capacity of over 62,000 people, making it the 3rd largest in English football and the largest stadium in London.
